Popular Drinks and Beverages of India

O

Falooda

Falooda is a milk-based cold dessert drink that is one of the legacy desserts passed down from the Mughals.

Kahwa

This drink is quite popular in India, Pakistan, Afghanistan and the trail of Kahwa goes as far as Turkey.

Bhang Thandai

The best time to enjoy a Bhang Thandai is during the Holi festival in India.

Chaang

This drink is commonly known as the beer of the Himalayas due to its availability from Jammu and Kashmir and Nepal in the North of India to Sikkim and Assam in the North East.

Masala Chai

the combination of spices typically varies from place to place, and the uniqueness of Masala Chai also lies in that the flavour changes with the mix of spices.

Panakam

Panakam is a jaggery and water-based drink which can be spiced up using cardamom, dry ginger, peppercorns, lemon juice and other ingredients to make it sweet and tangy.

Indian Slight Meal

O

Ganthiya

Ganthiya is a popular Indian deep-fried chickpea flour snack. Its origins are from Gujarat, India, where it is frequently served with tea. Ganthiya, unlike most other Indian snacks, is soft and fluffy rather than crispy.

Mathri

Matthi or Mathri is a delicious Indian cracker or biscuit in Punjab. Flour, salt, oil or ghee, and roughly powdered black peppercorns are typically used to make the crackers. To extend the shelf life of the crackers, store them in containers.

Masala Cashews

A bowl of toasted cashew nuts with ghee and just enough spice to please your Indian spirit. These masala-loaded cashews beg to be paired with an evening snack or coffee, thanks to a rich blend of spices, including acidic amchur.

Varites Of Sweets

O

Besan Ladoo

Besan Ladoo is a popular Indian sweet made from gram flour a.k.a besan, powdered sugar and clarified butter (ghee).

Ras malai

Ras malai is a popular dessert consisting of white cream, sugar, milk, and cardamom-flavored paneer cheese known as chhana.

Kesari Kheer

Kheer or payasam is an ancient Indian dessert, a creamy rice pudding that is made in several versions across the country.

Jol Bhora Sandesh

Sandesh is a traditional Indian sweet, served as a dessert and consists of fresh paneer,sugar,cardamom and saffron.

Specialty of Indian Cuisine

O

North

The main food of Northern state is Cuisine. The staple food of North India is wheat which is consumed in the forms of rotis or chapatis with sabzi or curry. Most of the North Indian people prefer vegetarian diet except the Kashmiri. Non-vegetarian dishes are famous as well which includes Mughlai.

South

There is not one but many famous dishes in South India including Pongal, Masala dosa, Chicken 65, Sambar, Pulihora, Appam, Upma, Hyderabadi biryani and many more. While India is known for its spicy, mouth watering and tangy dishes, South Indian meals ensure that there is a balance. Most South Indian dishes are all very subtle flavoured..

East

Some popular dishes are momos (steamed, meat- or vegetable-filled wontons) and Thukpa (a clear soup). Traditional Indian food contains fewer calories which may help one to control and maintain weight. Indian food embraces immunity and brain too. Good for heart, less saturated fat in traditional Indian food helps to preserve our hearts.

West

The main food of Westhern state Pork Vindaloo, Chicken Xacuti, Fish Curry, Bhelpuri, Thepla, Daal-Baati-Choorma, Laal Maas, and Ghewar. Western cooking emphasizes the use of seasonings and condiments, including ketchup, gravy, and mustard. Processed sauces seemed to be served more with Western food.
